🌀 Understanding Gravitational Waves

Gravitational Strain (h): A measure of how much space-time is stretched and squeezed as a gravitational wave passes. Detectors like LIGO measure this strain as changes in distance smaller than a proton’s width.

Chirp Mass: A special combination of the two black hole masses that determines how quickly the wave “chirps” (increases in frequency) before the merger.

Frequency Evolution: As the black holes spiral together, they lose energy via gravitational radiation — the frequency and amplitude both rise until they merge and “ring down.”

This visualization uses simplified models to help you explore how different black hole masses affect the waveform’s shape and timing.
