Einstein Field Equations & Einstein‚ÄìHilbert Action

click to expand

Classical Einstein field equations:

\(R_{\mu\nu} - \tfrac{1}{2} g_{\mu\nu} R = \kappa\, T_{\mu\nu}\)

This relates spacetime curvature (left side) to energy and momentum (right side). It is the starting point for all GR calculations in the simulator.

Einstein‚ÄìHilbert action:

\(S = \int \sqrt{-g}\,d^4x\;\left(\tfrac{1}{2\kappa} R + \mathcal{L}_{\text{matter}}\right)\)

The action encodes dynamics; varying it gives the field equations above. Useful as a basis for theoretical extensions and quantization approaches.

Quantum-corrected / Unified Extension

click to expand

\(G_{\mu\nu} = \kappa\big(T_{\mu\nu} + \mathcal{Q}_{\mu\nu}\big)\)

Here \(\mathcal{Q}_{\mu\nu}\) is a phenomenological stress‚Äìenergy-like tensor encoding quantum corrections or new fields (graviton couplings, dark-sector contributions). Turning on this term in simulation toggles small waveform and energy corrections.

Schwarzschild Geometry & Time Dilation

click to expand

Schwarzschild radius:

\(r_s = \dfrac{2GM}{c^2}\)

Schwarzschild metric:

\(ds^2 = -\left(1-\dfrac{r_s}{r}\right)c^2 dt^2 + \left(1-\dfrac{r_s}{r}\right)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega^2\)

Gravitational time dilation:

\(\Delta t' = \dfrac{\Delta t}{\sqrt{1 - \dfrac{2GM}{rc^2}}}\)

These govern local clock rates and radial geometry. In the simulator we use \(r_s\) to scale the visual horizon and the time-dilation factor to influence decay/time-dependent processes when requested.

Gravitational Waves (classical & quantum-corrected)

click to expand

Classical wave eqn:

\(\Box h_{\mu\nu} = \dfrac{16\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}\)

Quantum-corrected form:

\(\Box h_{\mu\nu} = \dfrac{16\pi G}{c^4}\big(T_{\mu\nu} + Q_{\mu\nu}\big)\)

We model the leading-order chirp using PN-informed formulas; \(Q_{\mu\nu}\) can be toggled to introduce small amplitude/phase shifts to study phenomenological effects.

Black-Hole Thermodynamics

click to expand

Hawking temperature:

\(T_H = \dfrac{\hbar c^3}{8\pi G M k_B}\)

Bekenstein‚ÄìHawking entropy:

\(S_{\mathrm{BH}} = \dfrac{k_B c^3 A}{4 G \hbar}\)

These semiclassical relations are used to set qualitative color/temperature mappings for the accretion disk and to inform energetic scaling in visualizations.

Particle Decay near BHs & Dark Matter Effects

click to expand

Base decay rate:

\(\Gamma_0 = \dfrac{\hbar}{\tau}\)

Gravity-corrected decay:

\(\Gamma = \Gamma_0 \left(1 - \dfrac{r_s}{r}\right)\)

Dark-matter modified:

\(\Gamma_{\rm dark} = \Gamma_0\big(1 + \alpha\,\rho_{\rm DM}\big)\)

Combined effective rate:

\(\Gamma_{\rm eff} = \Gamma_0\left(1 - \dfrac{r_s}{r}\right)\left(1 + \alpha\,\rho_{\rm DM}\right)\)

Use the calculators in this tab to produce numeric values for \(\Gamma_{\rm eff}\). These formulas will later be wired into the event visuals so decay-driven phenomena (color flicker, lifetime overlays) are consistent with the physics.

Primordial Black Hole (PBH) ‚Äî DM Profile

click to expand

PBH-modified dark matter:

\(\rho_{\rm DM}(r) = \dfrac{\rho_0}{\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{r_s}\right)^2}\)

This simple profile is useful for testing how localized PBHs change local \(\rho_{\rm DM}\) and therefore impact decay rates or local gravitational dynamics.

Gravitational Lensing / Deflection

\(\delta\theta \approx \dfrac{4GM}{c^2 r}\)

We use this relation to compute simple star-position shifts near the BH for the overview visual (subtle effect, used as an optional overlay).
